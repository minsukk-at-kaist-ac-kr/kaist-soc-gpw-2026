About Global Preview Week

KAIST SoC Global Preview Week is a short, intensive invitation program designed to introduce promising students to the research environment and graduate programs of KAIST School of Computing.

The main program will be held at KAIST in Daejeon, with a one-day cultural and networking visit to Seoul before departure. Participants will meet faculty members and graduate students, visit research labs, learn about graduate admissions and scholarships, and explore research internship or graduate study opportunities at KAIST.

KAIST will provide financial support for selected participants, covering round-trip airfare, accommodation, meals, and local program-related expenses during the official GPW period.

About School of Computing, KAIST

KAIST School of Computing is one of the leading computer science schools in Asia. Its faculty and students conduct research across a broad range of computing areas, including artificial intelligence, systems, cybersecurity, data science, software engineering, theory, human-computer interaction, graphics, and interdisciplinary computing.

The School offers a highly research-oriented environment for graduate study, with opportunities to work closely with faculty members and graduate students on internationally competitive research projects.

Program Goals

  • Introduce KAIST School of Computing research areas and graduate programs.
  • Help students explore research topics and possible faculty/lab matches.
  • Build long-term academic connections with leading universities in Asia.
  • Encourage future research internships and graduate applications to KAIST.

Who Should Be Nominated?

We welcome nominations of strong students who may be interested in graduate study or research internships at KAIST.

  • Advanced undergraduate students or master’s students.
  • Students in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, AI, Data Science, Cybersecurity, Electrical Engineering, Mathematics, or related fields.
  • Students with strong academic records and research potential.
  • Students who can participate in English and travel to Korea during the program period.

Selection Criteria

  • Academic excellence and preparation in computing-related fields.
  • Research potential and motivation for graduate study.
  • Fit with KAIST School of Computing research areas.
  • Availability for the full program period, including visa and travel feasibility.

Financial Support

There is no program fee for selected participants. KAIST will provide financial support covering round-trip airfare, accommodation, meals, and local program-related expenses during the official GPW period.

Personal expenses and costs outside the official program period will not be covered. Visa requirements and travel documents should be checked by each participant as early as possible after selection.

Nomination Process

GPW is designed as a nomination-based invitation program for selected partner universities. We ask each invited department or faculty contact to nominate 3–5 outstanding students.

Students formally nominated by their department will receive priority consideration. While KAIST will make the final selection, we expect that most nominated students who meet the eligibility requirements will be invited, subject to overall program capacity and visa/logistical constraints.

Expected materials from students

  • CV
  • Transcript
  • Research interests and motivation statement
  • Preferred KAIST research areas or labs, if any
  • Passport and visa-related information

Expected program size: approximately 20–30 students in total.
